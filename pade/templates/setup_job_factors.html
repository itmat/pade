Pick block and condition factors

Now you need to pick one factor to test for differential effects; this will be the "condition" factor. For the other factors, you have two options.

You can set them as "block" factors, which means that we will only compare the effects of the condition factor within each block. For example, if you have a blocking factor called "gender", and a condition called "treated", we will only compare treated males to untreated males, and treated females to untreated females.

You can also ignore other factors. In our example above, if we ignore "gender", it would be as if the factor didn't exist at all. We would compare all treated samples to all untreated samples, ignoring the effects of gender.
